How to Write a Feature Story for a Newspaper

Total time making story: Depends on what the turn around is
Time to conduct interviews: Maximum 2 hours

Writing a decent feature story for a newspaper can be quite tricky, but this guide is the ultimate way to be able to learn whatever you may be missing to write that perfect story

  • Things You'll Need

  • Some sort of notepad(digital or physical)
  • Interviewee(s)
  • Secondary sources
  • Information that is relevant to story

  1. Conduct some preliminary research on the topic/subject before contacting people

    Making sure that you have some prior knowledge on the topic/person(s) you are writing your story on is important, so that when you go to interview the person(s) involved in that subject, you understand more of what goes on.

  2. Find contact information of the person(s) and ask to interview them

    Typically the best method of contacting someone for an interview is by email, however if you feel that contacting them on social media or other platforms are best, than contact them there. Sometimes an email isn't always accessible while a social media page is.

    Interviewing in person is the best way to interview, as it allows for conversations to flow easier and be a little less pre-constructed.

  3. Conducting the Interview(s)

    When doing the interviews with everyone that you want to be involved with in the story, I personally recommend that you record audio of the conversation in addition to taking notes so that you can go back and listen to specific answers if you need to pick out certain quotes or talking points.

  4. Getting Ideas out

    Right after every interview that you conduct, it's a good idea to get some ideas from that interview out onto a piece of paper or a document so that when you go to fully write your story you have some ideas already in your head

  5. Writing Your Story

    Starting your story off with a strong lead is important for keeping readers interested and pulled into your story. From there you want to keep your story relatively concise with not too much filler, as it could bore readers. Adhering to AP style is also of high importance, as not adhering to it could result in your story getting turned down by newspapers

  6. Editing Your Story

    Once your story is completely written, it is recommended to go back and read through your story to look for any possible grammatical errors you made along the way, as well as any possible mistakes that don't align with AP style. Checking for any wordy sentences or long paragraphs to break up is also worth looking for.

  7. Your Story is Done!

    Your story is now done and ready to be turned in/published for the rest of the world to see!
